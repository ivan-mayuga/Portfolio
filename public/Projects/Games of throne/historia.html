⚔ The Chronicles of Westeros ⚔
I
Origins

A Song of Ice and Fire

Game of Thrones is based on the epic fantasy book series A Song of Ice and Fire by George R. R. Martin. While the story itself is entirely fictional, it draws heavily from real-world history, geography, and political dynamics — which is why it often feels so realistic and grounded.

II
The World

Westeros & Essos

The series takes place in two main fictional continents: Westeros and Essos. Westeros is where most of the central action happens — a land made up of competing noble houses, shifting alliances, and a constant struggle for power over the Iron Throne. Essos, on the other hand, is a vast and diverse continent to the east, featuring different cultures, cities, and political systems, adding depth to the world.

III
Historical Roots

The Wars of the Roses

Despite being a fantasy, many elements are inspired by real historical events. One of the biggest influences is the Wars of the Roses — a series of civil wars in 15th-century England between rival factions (the House of Lancaster and the House of York), which mirrors the power struggles between noble families in the series. The constant betrayals, shifting loyalties, and brutal fights for the throne reflect this period of instability.

"When you play the game of thrones, you win or you die. There is no middle ground."

— Cersei Lannister
IV
Medieval Influence

The Shadow of the Middle Ages

The setting is also influenced by medieval Europe more broadly. The castles, armor, feudal system, and monarchy seen in Game of Thrones closely resemble life during the Middle Ages. Even specific locations in the show were filmed in real places such as Northern Ireland, Croatia, and Iceland, which helped bring the fictional world to life with realistic landscapes.

V
Martin's Vision

Myth, Ambition & Moral Ambiguity

In addition to history, Martin also drew inspiration from mythology and classic fantasy, but he intentionally grounded the story in human conflict — politics, ambition, family loyalty, and moral ambiguity — rather than relying purely on magic. This blend of realism and fantasy is what makes Game of Thrones feel both imaginative and believable at the same time.

Source Material

Based on A Song of Ice and Fire, a series of novels by George R. R. Martin, begun in 1996.

🏰

Real Filming Locations

Filmed across Northern Ireland, Croatia, Iceland, Spain, and Malta to create Westeros on screen.

⚔

Historical Parallel

The Wars of the Roses (1455–1487) in England were a primary real-world inspiration for the saga's power struggles.

🌍

Two Continents

The world is divided between Westeros in the west and the vast continent of Essos to the east, each with unique cultures.
